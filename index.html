01. Motivation & Research

1.1 The Why: Choosing the Active Track

Our team chose the Active Track because we recognized a fundamental gap in how fitness is approached within university environments like XJTLU. While students are increasingly health-conscious, the "Active" lifestyle often remains unoptimized due to a lack of scientific guidance. FitMatrix was born from the desire to demystify complex physiological data. We chose this track specifically to leverage software engineering to provide real-time, actionable insights that traditional methods overlook. By focusing on Active technology, we can address the "gym anxiety" and "training plateaus" that often lead to high dropout rates among beginners. Our platform ensures that physical activity is purposeful, data-driven, and engaging. We believe that by creating a scientific feedback loop—using MET calculations and real-time occupancy tracking—we can empower students to move from intuitive training to precise, high-performance habits. Ultimately, the Active Track allowed us to explore the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Sports Science, creating a digital coach that fosters a healthier, more informed, and scientifically-active campus community.

1.2 The Gap: Academic & Commercial Analysis

Paper 01: Lyons et al. (2014)

  • ✔ Proved self-monitoring efficacy
  • ✔ Validated tracking techniques
  • ✔ Behavior change framework
  • ✖ Ignored visual goal-setting
  • ✖ No real-time environment data
  • ✖ High user entry barrier

Paper 02: Haynes (2009)

  • ✔ Identified choice overload
  • ✔ Minimized decision effort
  • ✔ Booking loop efficiency
  • ✖ Missed spatial data integration
  • ✖ Rigid delivery models
  • ✖ Limited to offline check-in

Paper 03: Zayim et al. (2023)

  • ✔ Cognitive task analysis
  • ✔ Identified data intimidation
  • ✔ Focus on health records
  • ✖ No real-world gym testing
  • ✖ Missed flexible service options
  • ✖ Lacked empathetic UI design

Paper 04: Cselik et al. (2026)

  • ✔ Environment-motivation link
  • ✔ Public health perspective
  • ✔ Focus on fitness centers
  • ✖ No software-based solution
  • ✖ Ignored "at-home" flexibility
  • ✖ Static occupancy analysis

Product 01: Keep

  • ✔ Massive content library
  • ✔ Strong community platform
  • ✔ Professional video guides
  • ✖ Intimidating BMI/BF numbers
  • ✖ Solitary workout fatigue
  • ✖ No live gym load data

Product 02: Leke

  • ✔ Seamless offline check-in
  • ✔ Self-service automation
  • ✔ Pay-as-you-go model
  • ✖ Physical "Blind Box" experience
  • ✖ No at-home private training
  • ✖ Social anxiety during peak

Product 03: Xunji

  • ✔ Hardcore data recording
  • ✔ Weight progression tracking
  • ✔ "Quantified-Self" focus
  • ✖ Extreme cognitive load
  • ✖ Poor beginner empathy
  • ✖ No real-time spatial data

Product 04: Supermonkey

  • ✔ Immersive boutique design
  • ✔ Efficient O2O booking
  • ✔ No aggressive hard-selling
  • ✖ Equipment unavailability
  • ✖ Rigid store-only service
  • ✖ Unpredictable congestion
