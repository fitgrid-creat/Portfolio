Motivation &
Research.

Addressing macro-societal health trends through domain expertise.

The Why: Choosing the Active Track

We selected the Active track driven by a dual motivation: addressing urgent macro-societal health trends and leveraging our team's micro-level domain expertise.

First, from a macro-demand perspective, public health awareness has surged dramatically. 'Staying active' has transitioned from a niche, hardcore hobby into a universal lifestyle goal. However, despite this growing enthusiasm, there remains a glaring gap for highly empathetic, user-centric applications. Current market solutions typically rely on complex, overwhelming numerical data—such as rigid calorie counting and advanced physiological metrics—that actively intimidate beginners. The broader public urgently needs intuitive, approachable software that lowers the cognitive barrier to scientific exercise, fostering long-term adherence rather than short-term anxiety.

Second, on a micro-level, our team possesses a profound, lived understanding of this specific domain. Compared to the Heritage or Social tracks, we are active fitness participants ourselves. We firmly believe that the most resonant user experiences are crafted when developers intimately understand their users' daily struggles. Operating simultaneously as both creators and target users grants us genuine empathy. This deep familiarity allows us to accurately capture real-world pain points—such as performance data anxiety, decision fatigue, and gym crowding—enabling us to design a product that authentically aligns with the public's practical needs and transforms exercise from a daunting task into a seamless, visual experience.

Academic Gaps: Literature Review

Theoretical Pros vs Gaps

1. Visualizing Goals

(X. Zheng et al, 2025)
+ Theoretical Pros

Confirmed that visual design significantly lowers cognitive barriers; demonstrated that app-based health empowerment enhances long-term adherence; provides cutting-edge theoretical support for setting goals via intuitive outcome images.

- Academic Gaps

Lacks practical details on specific visual interaction forms; ignores the expectation gap caused by users' varying genetic differences; fails to explore how online visual goals integrate with offline gym services.

2. Crowding & Anxiety

(A. R. et al, 2023)
+ Theoretical Pros

Rigorously quantified the destructive impact of "perceived crowding" on exercise motivation; clearly stated that high spatial density triggers social anxiety; theoretically justified the necessity of "real-time crowdedness tracking" from an environmental psychology perspective.

- Academic Gaps

Proposed solutions rely too heavily on passive crowd control by gym operators; lacks discussion on proactive interventions using mobile app data; fails to differentiate crowding levels across various functional zones within the gym.

3. Spatial Barriers

(S. Kim and Y. Cho, 2023)
+ Theoretical Pros

Identified dual constraints of time and space as core barriers to visiting physical gyms; verified the commercial potential of home fitness models in sustaining exercise routines; supported the logic of "at-home private training" to break physical space barriers.

- Academic Gaps

Limited solely to one-way screen-based video workouts; overlooks the psychological inertia users develop without supervision while exercising alone at home; completely lacks exploration of the highly flexible O2O (online-to-offline) at-home coaching model.

4. Decision Friction

(D. A. et al, 2020)
+ Theoretical Pros

Proved that goal-setting mechanisms crucially guide health behaviors based on massive user datasets; confirmed that reducing initial decision friction greatly boosts user activity; highlighted the critical role of lowering心理 barriers in overcoming exercise resistance.

- Academic Gaps

Research still focuses on traditional, abstract metrics like calories; severely lacks comparative analysis of "image-based/non-quantified" goal-setting methods; fails to create a closed-loop system connecting app goals with real-world physical spatial experiences.

Algorithm &
Theory.

A peer-reviewed biomechanical engine based on absolute clinical metrics.

This decision engine is not a "black-box tool" based on subjective experience, but an advanced sports medical algorithm model that transforms absolute clinical physical quantities of modern exercise physiology (e.g., minutes, sets) into relative volume percentages through biostatistical "Reference Extremum Modeling", "Zero-Sum MED Constraining", and "Dose-Response Scaling". This document extracts the 5 core mathematical designs underlying the engine and their corresponding top-tier peer-reviewed literature support.

Coordinate Origin (50:50)

Setting 50% : 50% (Resistance:Cardio) as an absolutely neutral mathematical origin prevents systematic bias during subsequent linear coefficient superpositions.

Hawley (2009): The zero-sum game of endurance and hypertrophy requires precise homeostasis management.

Hickson (1980): Classic 1:1 volume control model for concurrent training.

García-Pallarés (2011): Even distribution is necessary during the preparatory phase to lay the foundation.

Denominator Normalization

Extracting the physical extremum (300 mins/week) from international authoritative guidelines as the algorithmic "denominator" to convert discrete absolute time into rigorous mathematical proportions.

WHO (2020): The extremum for maximum adult health benefits is 300+ mins/week.

Donnelly et al. (2009): The benchmark extremum for clinically significant weight loss is >250 mins/week.

Clamping Functions

To prevent system collapse and overtraining, extreme clamping functions are introduced: hard-locking the resistance ceiling to 85% and the cardio budget ceiling to 60%.

Wilson et al. (2012): Hypertrophy is significantly reduced when cardio >3 days/week, defining a 30% interference red line.

Dose-Response Coefficients

Using linear multipliers and MED (Minimum Effective Dose) mechanisms to control volume: hypertrophy budget expands linearly by 12%; fat-loss resistance yields strictly by 4% (protecting lean mass); HIIT dynamically scales up to 25% to combat metabolic adaptation.

Slentz / Schoenfeld: Confirmed significant linear trends between volume and effect.

Spiering (2021): Defined the Minimum Effective Dose (MED) red line for maintaining lean mass.

Tremblay / Boutcher: Confirmed metabolic adaptation phenomena and the extremum golden time window.

Micro Cardio Splitting

Micro-ratios are strictly bound to clinical protocols: 80/20 in hypertrophy phases avoids "junk miles"; recomposition phases allocate 100% to steady-state Zone 2 to limit central nervous system fatigue.

Seiler / Barakat: Confirmed the necessity of polarized and highly conservative LISS strategies.

Social & Ethical Implications

Responsibilities

Health Data Privacy

The system collects sensitive data such as the user's current body type, ideal body type, exercise habits, and frequency of visits to the gym. If the data storage is not secure and is used for analysis or advertising without the user's consent, it will infringe upon user privacy and pose a risk of health data leakage. Therefore, we are committed to continuously maintaining the system with strict encryption protocols to protect user privacy.

Safety in Health Advice

The system offers automatically generated weekly training plans, but they are not evaluated one-on-one by doctors or professional coaches. For users with injuries, chronic diseases, or weak constitutions, there may be risks associated with exercise. We acknowledge this limitation and will implement mandatory liability disclaimers and recommend consulting professionals for users with specific health conditions.

Technical Reflection

Methodology

We utilized Gemini to accelerate the development of our frontend components and specific interactive logic. To ensure the output met our design and functional requirements, we used highly specific prompts across three key areas:

  • •
    Core Frontend UI: We directed the AI to generate a layout strictly following a "dark mode glassmorphism" aesthetic. The prompt explicitly required the use of deep black backgrounds, translucent surface layers, and smooth transition animations for all hover effects to create a premium visual experience.
  • •
    3x3 Fitness Matrix Selector (Core Logic): We instructed the AI to build an interactive 3x3 matrix component for selecting workout intensity. Our prompt enforced three strict functional requirements: (1) highlighting clicked cells with a glowing accent color, (2) saving the selected value into a state variable, and (3) rendering a "Generate Plan" button below the grid that remains inactive until a cell is selected.
  • •
    Real-Time Gym Crowding Detection: To handle live data, we prompted the AI to generate the necessary API interfaces and logic for real-time data transmission, simulating the live gym occupancy updates.

Multiple verification methods were adopted to ensure the AI-generated code fully met user requirements, including conducting functional testing to examine whether core features such as personalised fitness planning, group time selection, real-time gym crowd checking and private lesson booking operated as intended, alongside user requirement checklist validation to align developed functions with real needs gathered from questionnaires and interviews, with unreasonable logic revised and optimised iteratively throughout the process.
